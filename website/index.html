Features

Tunnels that manage themselves

Built for anyone who reaches databases, dashboards, and internal services over SSH. Fast, keyboard-friendly, security-first, and fully offline.

On-Demand Connect

The SSH session stays closed until something actually touches the local port. First byte in, Jump Hippo dials the connection — the listener itself is always bound and ready.

Idle Teardown

When the last connection closes and the port goes quiet, the SSH session is torn down automatically — no lingering sessions or wasted sockets. The listener stays bound, ready to reconnect.

Jump-Host Chains

Reach hosts you can't hit directly. Chain any number of bastions and jump hosts; Jump Hippo hops through them in order and forwards to the final destination.

Pause & Resume

Pause a tunnel to drop its live SSH session without deleting the definition. Resume any time — the port stays bound and reconnects on the next request.

Live Monitoring

Watch live upload/download rates, peaks and averages, byte totals, connection counts, and uptime update in real time — as drag-and-drop cards you arrange yourself or a sortable table. Pick exactly which metrics each view shows.

Password or Key Auth

Authenticate each hop with a password or an SSH private key and passphrase. Secrets are encrypted at rest, and the UI never sees a decrypted credential.

Host-Key Verification

Every SSH host is checked against known_hosts. First contact prompts for explicit trust — Jump Hippo never silently accepts an unknown key.

Local-Only by Default

Listeners bind to 127.0.0.1 out of the box — reachable only from your own machine. Exposing a tunnel to your LAN is a deliberate opt-in: you enter an explicit address:port, never the default.

Local, Reverse & Dynamic

Forward however you need: classic local forwards, reverse forwards that expose a service from your side, and dynamic forwarding that turns a tunnel into a SOCKS5 proxy — same on-demand lifecycle for all three.

Encrypted, Your Way

Choose how secrets are stored at rest: a promptless device key, your OS keychain, or a master password (PBKDF2 → AES-256-GCM, held only in memory). Switching backends re-encrypts everything at once — and no mode ever downgrades a secret to plaintext.

Groups & Bulk Actions

Sort tunnels into colour-coded, collapsible groups — Work, Home lab, whatever fits. Arm, disarm, or pause an entire group in one click from its header, the tray, or the menu. Ungrouped tunnels stay first-class.

Signed & Cross-Platform

Native builds for macOS, Windows, and Linux on both arm64 and x64 — signed and notarized where the platform supports it, with non-forced in-app auto-update.
